Web Site Critiques

Question List:

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Trader Joe's

https://www.traderjoes.com/home

My first impression of the website is that it's pretty well designed. It looks good, and isn't to complicated. They did a great job organizing the website. They have links at the top for stores, links on the left for products, and different types of recipes. They did a good job differentiating between the links and the information on the home page. The structure of the site makes sense. They have announcements at the top, new recipes, and new products. They ordered them in a way thats easy to read and understand. The images and graphics help with the transitions from each section. It fits their brand, and looks good. The load time was very quick, loaded in less than a second. The text size and color are very easy to read. Even on a bigger monitor, I have no issues reading it. When they use an image for the background of text, all it does is slightly change the color, nothing overlaps with the text. This makes it very readable. The website does have a navigation tool, in the form of a search bar. However clicking on it moves it the center of the top of the screen which seems a little weird. But it works as intended. Most of the site has a white background, with black text. For titles and new sections they make the text red and add images. They also add heading breaks in good spots. The site responds well to making the window smaller. All the same information is present and available when you make it smaller. Something that I thought they did well was have all their links at the bottom of the website. They listed them in a nice way making it super easy to find the information you need.
